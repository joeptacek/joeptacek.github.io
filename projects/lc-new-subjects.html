--- layout: project title: LC New Subjects ---

LC New Subjects is a Twitter bot for staying in touch with the latest subject heading updates from the Library of Congress (LC). LC releases these updates en masse, approximately once a month; LC New Subjects gathers these up and gradually Tweets its way through the heap.

My basic goal with this project is to help updates from LC reach a broader audience. A partial listing of these updates is published on the LC website; a more complete index is also available, but it's a bit harder to find. By using Twitter to rebroadcast these lists, I hope to make it easier for librarians (and non-librarians) to monitor LC decision-making. I'm also hoping to create space for ongoing reflection and conversation regarding LC subject heading terminology—since Twitter is a public forum, anyone can share or add comments on updates that resonate in one way or another, e.g., the recent decision to finally cancel the "Illegal aliens" subject heading.

Under the hood, LC New Subjects depends on two Python scripts—one for scraping the LC website, and another for programmatically sending out Tweets. I've documented and published relevant code in two GitHub repositories, lc-scrape and lc-tweet.
